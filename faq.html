Frequently Asked Questions

General

SASE is the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers. Founded in 2007, this organization helps Asian heritage students and professionals achieve their full potential while also promoting diversity and inclusion.. There are chapters of SASE across many different universities, including the Pennsylvania State University.

SASE organizes various events, such as conferences, career fairs, workshops, and networking opportunities, where members can connect with professionals, industry leaders, and like-minded individuals. These events provide valuable resources for career development, mentorship, and building professional connections. Additionally, SASE offers scholarships, leadership development programs, and other initiatives to support its members throughout their educational and professional journeys. The organization is dedicated to empowering Asian heritage scientists and engineers and fostering their success in the STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields.

Dues for club membership are $15 per school year, either in the fall or the spring.

The point system is a way to track how active a member is in SASE. By simply attending GBM's, workshops, socials, and other club events, you are able to earn points in an easy way.

To be considered an active member, all you need to do is pay dues and attend any SASE events you can! The more active you are, the more you'll be able to network with others, form friendships, and hone your professional skills.

Being an active member gives you automatic discounts to various places downtown, elgibility to run for E-Board positions, and one of the first chosen to go to National and Regional Conference. You also have the opportunity to apply to be a chair for the Events Director as well, gaining leadership experience in the process.

Conference

The National Conference is an annual event organized by SASE in the United States. It is the largest gathering of Asian heritage scientists and engineers, providing a platform for networking, professional development, and knowledge sharing. The conference features keynote speeches from industry leaders, workshops, panels, a career fair, networking events, and competitions. Attendees have the opportunity to connect with peers, professionals, and recruiters, explore job and internship opportunities, enhance their professional skills, and receive mentorship. The SASE National Conference promotes diversity, inclusion, and empowerment within the STEM community and serves as an inspiring and supportive environment for Asian heritage scientists and engineers to thrive.

To be chosen to go to National Conference, you must go through our application process. The application form is available on the SASE Instagram or GroupMe, or you can visit our Conference page for more details. The more active you are in the organization, the more likely we are to consider you for Nationals! Registration opens up in the summer.

The NorthEast Regional Conference (NERC) is the largest of the SASE regional conferences, consistently averaging approximately 500 students in attendance. It is the only regional conference to be planned and hosted exclusively by the Northeast Region as a collective of volunteers—a team of 16 students & alumni alongside the Regional Programing Coordinator— as opposed to a team at a single university. It offers a diverse variety of workshops, keynote speakers, networking events, and a career fair. These programs focus on educating and training AAPI students and professionals to empower our community to strive for diversity, inclusion, and in AAPI leadership in the workplace and beyond. Registration opens up in the spring!

National Conference is held in the fall, where SASE members and chapters from all around the country attend. NERC is held in the spring, and exclusive to SASE chapters and members in the Northeastern region, so it is smaller in scale.
